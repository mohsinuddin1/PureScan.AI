  <h3 className="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-4">Why do abrasive body scrubs and loofahs make strawberry skin and body bumps worse?</h3>
  <p>One of the most common mistakes people make when trying to smooth rough upper arms or back bumps is aggressively scrubbing with walnut shell polishes, apricot seed scrubs, or abrasive synthetic loofahs. While physical scrubbing temporarily removes surface flakes, dermatological studies reveal that jagged particles create micro-lacerations around hair follicles. This mechanical friction triggers localized follicular inflammation and hyperkeratosis—causing the skin to overproduce keratin as a defense mechanism, which forms even harder, redder plugs around each hair follicle.</p>
  
  <h3 className="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-4">How do heavy synthetic waxes and comedogenic body lotions trap keratin inside pores?</h3>
  <p>Many popular body moisturizers and scented lotions rely on heavy synthetic waxes and fatty esters such as Isopropyl Myristate, PEG-100 Stearate, and Myristyl Myristate to create a thick, creamy texture. While suitable for extremely dry heels or elbows, applying these comedogenic films across dense follicular zones like the upper arms, shoulders, and back prevents naturally shed corneocytes from escaping pore openings. Trapped beneath an occlusive layer, keratin plugs fuse with sebum to create stubborn keratosis pilaris bumps and inflammatory body acne.</p>
  
  <h3 className="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-4">How do high-glycemic snacks and dairy additives fuel body breakouts from the inside out?</h3>
  <p>Topical body care is only half the equation; internal endocrine signals dictate follicular turnover. Consuming ultra-processed snacks with high glycemic loads or protein bars loaded with concentrated milk solids and whey isolates elevates blood insulin and Insulin-Like Growth Factor 1 (IGF-1). Elevated IGF-1 directly stimulates sebaceous glands to produce thicker sebum and accelerates keratinocyte multiplication inside hair ducts. This internal biochemical cascade turns mild follicular congestion into persistent, inflamed red bumps across the back and chest.</p>
