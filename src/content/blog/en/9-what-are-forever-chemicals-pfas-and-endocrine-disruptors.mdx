  <h3 className="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-4">Why are PFAS and phthalates dangerous for your hormones and metabolism?</h3>
  <p>Endocrine-disrupting chemicals (EDCs) mimic or block natural hormones like estrogen, testosterone, and thyroid hormones. Even at microscopic doses, synthetic compounds such as phthalates and perfluoroalkoxy alkanes can confuse cellular receptors. Clinical studies indicate that chronic accumulation of these toxins slows metabolism, disrupts reproductive health, and contributes to cellular inflammation and insulin resistance over time.</p>
  
  <h3 className="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-4">Which everyday food packaging and skincare products secretly contain forever chemicals?</h3>
  <p>In skincare and cosmetics, PFAS are frequently added to waterproof mascara, long-wear foundation, and matte lipsticks to create a smooth, water-resistant finish—often disguised under technical chemical names like PTFE or perfluorodecalin. In the food industry, grease-resistant fast-food wrappers, microwave popcorn bags, and recycled cardboard boxes frequently leach fluorinated compounds directly into warm food.</p>

Without specialized toxicology training, even the most health-conscious shopper can easily overlook harmful synthetic additives.</p>
