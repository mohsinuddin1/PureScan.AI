  <h3 className="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-4">Why do chemical foot peel masks with high-concentration acids and harsh alcohols damage the plantar skin barrier?</h3>
  <p>Across Europe and the Netherlands, chemical foot peel booties have surged in popularity as a quick fix for calluses and dry feet. However, these masks frequently combine high percentages of glycolic, lactic, and salicylic acids with high-proof Denatured Alcohol (Alcohol Denat.) to force rapid desquamation. While this sheds dead keratinocytes, it simultaneously strips essential intercorneal ceramides and natural moisturizing factors (NMFs) from healthy skin tissue. This indiscriminate chemical burn leaves the freshly exposed plantar epidermis fragile, hyper-sensitive, and highly prone to deep, painful heel fissures under daily walking pressure.</p>
  <h3 className="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-4">Why do conventional antifungal nail lacquers and aggressive urea pastes fail to restore healthy nail keratin?</h3>
  <p>When toenails become thick, yellow, or detached, consumers often resort to over-the-counter antifungal lacquers or high-dose urea pastes (>40%). While keratolytic urea softens thickened plates, synthetic solvent carriers and harsh isopropyl alcohol in many topical lacquers macerate the delicate periungual nail fold. Without physiological barrier lipid support or breathable moisture regulation, the surrounding skin becomes inflamed and susceptible to secondary bacterial ingress, while the dermatophyte fungus remains entrenched within deep subungual keratin layers.</p>
  <h3 className="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-4">How do high-sugar diets, refined carbohydrates, and zinc or vitamin D deficiencies fuel fungal overgrowth from within?</h3>
  <p>Fungal organisms such as dermatophytes and *Candida* thrive on systemic glucose availability. Consuming diets rich in ultra-processed snacks, sugary beverages, and refined carbohydrates creates chronic blood sugar spikes that elevate skin tissue glucose, providing an abundant energy substrate for fungal proliferation. Additionally, deficiencies in essential micronutrients like zinc, vitamin D3, and bioavailable iron compromise localized cutaneous immune surveillance, weakening the nail matrix's ability to synthesize compact, pathogen-resistant beta-keratin.</p>
