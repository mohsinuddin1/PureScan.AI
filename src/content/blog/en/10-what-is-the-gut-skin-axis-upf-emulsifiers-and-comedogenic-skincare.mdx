  <h3 className="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-4">Why do food emulsifiers and UPF additives cause bloating and skin inflammation?</h3>
  <p>Emulsifiers are chemical binding agents used in processed foods to blend water and oils together. Clinical gastroenterology studies reveal that industrial emulsifiers like polysorbate 80, carrageenan, and carboxymethyl cellulose act like detergents on your intestinal lining. They break down the protective mucosal layer, allowing bacterial endotoxins to enter the bloodstream (leaky gut). This systemic inflammatory surge directly overstimulates sebaceous glands in the skin, leading to chronic hormonal breakouts, rosacea, and facial puffiness.</p>
  
  <h3 className="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-4">What comedogenic (pore-clogging) ingredients secretly hide in clean beauty and makeup?</h3>
  <p>Even products marketed as 'clean', 'natural', or 'organic' can harbor heavy comedogenic ingredients that cause acne. Common pore-clogging culprits include isopropyl myristate, algae extract, acetylated lanolin, and fractionated coconut oil derivatives. When applied to acne-prone skin, these heavy lipids form an occlusive film that traps bacteria and dead cells within follicles, triggering micro-comedones and cystic blemishes.</p>
