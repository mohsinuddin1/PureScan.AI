  <h3 className="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-4">Why is my laundry detergent causing a full-body rash?</h3>
  <p>Most commercial laundry detergents are designed to clean clothes quickly, not to protect your skin barrier. To make clothes look "cleaner" and smell fresh for weeks, brands load their formulas with synthetic fragrance mixes, preservatives (like methylisothiazolinone), and optical brighteners. Optical brighteners are fluorescent chemicals that absorb UV light and emit blue light to make yellowing fabrics appear white. They do not wash away; they are specifically engineered to remain bonded to your clothes. Constant physical contact with these synthetic residues, especially where clothing rubs tightly against the skin (waistbands, underarms, and thighs), triggers a severe immune response leading to Allergic Contact Dermatitis.</p>
  <h3 className="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-4">How do sweaty workouts make laundry rashes worse?</h3>
  <p>You might notice that your body rashes and itching peak during or after a workout. When you exercise, your pores open up, and the moisture from your sweat acts as a solvent. This sweat breaks down the dried chemical residues lingering in your workout gear. Harsh anionic surfactants (like SLS) and synthetic fragrances leach out of the fabric fibers and are drawn directly into your vulnerable skin. This chemical exposure strips away your skin's natural oils and violently disrupts the acid mantle, resulting in sudden hives, extreme chafing, and painful eczema flare-ups exactly where your clothes touch your body.</p>
  <h3 className="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-4">Are fabric softeners and dryer sheets really that dangerous for my skin?</h3>
  <p>Fabric softeners and dryer sheets are arguably the worst offenders for sensitive skin. Unlike detergents that are meant to be rinsed out, softeners are explicitly designed to coat your clothing in a waxy, water-resistant film. This film is primarily made of Quaternary Ammonium Compounds (Quats) and synthetic artificial lipids. While they make fabrics feel soft and reduce static, these artificial coatings severely impair the breathability of your clothes. They trap body heat, lock in sweat, and provoke severe skin sensitivities, effectively suffocating your skin in a layer of chemical allergens.</p>
