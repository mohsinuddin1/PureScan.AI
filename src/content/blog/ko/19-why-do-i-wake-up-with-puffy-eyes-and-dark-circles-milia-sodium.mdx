  <h3 className="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-4">Why do rich eye creams cause tiny white keratin bumps (milia) around the eyes?</h3>
  <p>One of the most frequent surprises for people using rich anti-aging eye creams is the sudden appearance of small, pearly white bumps known as milia. The periorbital skin surrounding the eyes is exceptionally thin and lacks dense sebaceous glands. When heavy occlusive emollients such as Lanolin, Paraffin, Mineral Oil, or Beeswax are applied around this delicate contour, they impede normal skin desquamation. Trapped keratin flakes accumulate beneath the outer epidermis, hardening into tiny white cysts that cannot be easily extracted without professional care.</p>
  
  <h3 className="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-4">How do migrating fragrances and preservatives cause under-eye dark circles and irritation?</h3>
  <p>Even when applied carefully along the orbital bone, eye creams and facial serums warm up and migrate across the skin during sleep. Formulas containing synthetic fragrance, benzyl alcohol, or harsh preservative systems (such as Methylisothiazolinone or Diazolidinyl Urea) can seep into the conjunctiva and tear film. This nightly chemical exposure triggers low-grade allergic contact dermatitis and vascular dilation. Over time, chronic micro-inflammation leads to post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation, casting a persistent brownish or purplish shadow beneath the eyes.</p>
  
  <h3 className="text-xl font-bold text-slate-900 mt-8 mb-4">How do high-sodium dinners, MSG, and ultra-processed snacks trigger morning eye bags?</h3>
  <p>Under-eye bags aren't strictly a cosmetic issue; they reflect overnight osmotic fluid dynamics. When you consume evening meals or snacks high in sodium chloride, Monosodium Glutamate (MSG), or dehydrating additives, blood plasma osmolality rises. To dilute excess sodium, your body retains water in extracellular tissues. Because horizontal sleep positioning eliminates gravitational drainage, this excess interstitial fluid pools in the loose, highly compliant connective tissue beneath the lower eyelids, leaving you with noticeable puffiness upon waking.</p>
